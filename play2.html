HESTER.

Do you, in England, allow no friendship to exist between a young man and a young girl?

[_Enter_ LADY HUNSTANTON, _followed by Footman with shawls and a cushion_.]
LADY CAROLINE.

We think it very inadvisable. Jane, I was just saying what a pleasant party you have asked us to meet. You have a wonderful power of selection. It is quite a gift.

HESTER.

Dear Caroline, how kind of you! I think we all do fit in very nicely together. And I hope our charming American visitor will carry back pleasant recollections of our English country life. [_To Footman_.] The cushion, there, Francis. And my shawl. The Shetland. Get the Shetland. [_Exit Footman for shawl_.]
